The article on semantic HTML was a great summary of the importance of HTML, as well as a good reference for the different semantic HTML tags. Through clarifying the content of a page using semantic tags, we as designers are able to organize the information on a webpage so that it is accessible to everyone.

In the recent article I wrote and styled, I made use of many different semantic tags. In particular, I used the "figure" tag for the first time. It allowed me to set apart my image elements, as I was able to use the tag "figcaption" to separate the image captions from the normal text of the article. Other useful semantic tags that I used included "blockquote", "nav", and "section". Because my article was so long, having these semantic tags helped tremendously with my organization.

Because the topic of my article was on Peter Paul’s Ruben’s painting Samson and Delilah, I wanted the styling to reflect the subject matter. I chose a neutral palette, as I felt that was well suited for an art history piece, and ensured that my text was easily legible, as there was a lot of text for the reader to get through. I found that the most challenging part of visual design is choosing the best color palette, as it is difficult for me to choose color combinations that are harmonious.
